Introduction

The New York Times has had the Spelling Bee puzzle in the print version of the weekly New York Times Magazine since 2014. The daily web version launched on May 9, 2018. Wikipedia has links to several stories about the game and the vibrant community of people who enjoy it. Many people play the game on their phone with the New York Times app.

Here is the NY Times web version:

    nytimes.com/puzzles/spelling-bee
Now there's another way to play:
    logicalpoetry.com/nytbee
This new way has several advantages. There is a short film that shows how it works. Give it a try and let me know what you think.

How to Play 

     T   F
   E   N   Y
     L   U
  • Create words using the 7 letters.
  • Words must contain at least 4 letters.
  • Words must include the center letter.
  • Letters can be used more than once.

  • The list of allowed words does not include words that are obscure, hyphenated, or proper nouns.
  • No cussing either, sorry.

  • Score points to increase your rating.
  • 4-letter words are worth 1 point each.
  • Longer words earn 1 point per letter.
  • Each puzzle includes at least one "pangram" which uses every letter. These are worth 7 extra points!

Words and Commands

In the text field you enter words that satisfy the rules of the puzzle. You can enter more than one word at a time.

Pressing Return will clear any messages and permute the 6 outer letters.

There are also several commands you can enter in the text field:

IShow details about the puzzle including numbers of words, points, and pangrams. If the puzzle was created by the hivemind community information from the creator is given.
RShow the rankings. It also shows how many points are needed to get to the next rank.
WShow the found words in the order they were found.
C  YClear the puzzle and start over.


Commands that give hints:

HTShow the Hint Table.
TLShow the Two Letter List.

DPDefine the Pangrams.
DB4Define the words beginning with B of length 4.
DTRDefine the words beginning with TR.
D <word>  Define the word. No hints tallied.
DA ...The D command gives at most 3 definitions. DA will give them All.
Definitions come from 3 dictionaries: Merriam-Webster, Collins, and Oxford. With Community Puzzles if there is a clue for a word it is shown instead of the dictionary definition.

V2PReveal the first two letters of the pangrams.
V3M8Reveal the first 3 letters of the words beginning with M of length 8.
V5GLReveal the first 5 letters of the words beginning with GL.
Using E instead of V reveals letters at the front and at the End of the word. Commands that reveal the entire word are silently ignored (as are commands like V1M8 or V2GL).
Both define and reveal commands skip words that have already been found.

1Show a random entry from the Hint Table.
2Show a random entry from the Two Letter List.
These will work even if the Table and List are not shown.


Commands to access the large NY Times Archive of Spelling Bee puzzles and the puzzles created by the Hivemind Community:

NRNew puzzle from a Random date.
N <date>New puzzle from the given date. You can enter dates in several ways: 10-20-19 or 9-23 (current year) or 15 (current month). Dates must be on or after May 29, 2018.
TReturn to Today's puzzle.
CP<num>New Community Puzzle #num.
LList the current puzzles.
P<num>Return to puzzle #num in the list.
X<nums>Remove the numbered puzzles from the list. You can give a range. For example: X2 4-6 9   This clears the words you found so if you choose the date again you will start afresh.

Hint Strategy

Many people who solve the puzzle try to use as few hints as possible. The ultimate is to achieve QBABM = Queen Bee all by myself. This is possible but rather difficult. There are many ways to get hints and each increases your 'hint total'. The number of hints are tallied like so:

    Command  # hints
    HT10
    TL5
    D,DA3for each word
    V,E2for each word
    11
    21
    G Y5for each word

Power User Commands

Limited use ..., it's fun to add functionality for the simple joy of programming! With a text command oriented interface one doesn't have to fiddle endlessly with the graphic interface and fret about "user friendliness". Users will adapt to the idea of single (or two) letter command words.
    SCShow how the score was tallied and how many words remain to be found.
    FFind other puzzles that use the same seven letters.
    S  <word>Find other puzzles where the word is used.
    R%Show the rankings with the percentage of the maximum score.
    W>Show the found words in order of increasing length.
    W>nShow the found words of length greater than n.
    W<Show the found words in order of decreasing length.
    W<nShow the found words of length less than n.
    WnShow the found words of length n.
    W  <prefix>Show the found words that start with <prefix>.
    G  YGive up and show the words you have not yet found.

Other NYT Bee Projects

The NY Times Spelling Bee concept has been a fertile source of challenge for many clever software engineers.

Games You Can Play

swilliams.io.fun/hex - A Javascript implementation. Fast. Clean code. Uses files of 1500 seven pangramic words and a 10,000 word file of common words. A different puzzle each day. No ranks. Not NYT Puzzles so not 'curated'. No hint table.

freebee.fun is a free web version of the game that you can play! Javascript and HTML/CSS. It doesn't use NYT words so the word list is not 'curated'. There is no hint table. Source code in C is available for a command line version.

Philip Shemella A command line game you can play written in Python. Again, doesn't use NYT words. It also generates new games.

NY Times Puzzle Answers

The 7 sites below show the list of allowed words in the NY Times puzzle for each day. In a variety of formats. The intention is to help people solve the puzzle or, more accurately, see what words they missed. Most have annoying ads. 😞
  1. nytbee.com by Chris McLeester. This site was the main source for the archive. Historical analysis of the puzzles and nice graphs. No ads 👍.
  2. shun.net/bee Optionally shows the table of hints and the 2 letter counts. An entertaining FAQ.
  3. hejhej.dev/bee Today and yesterday only. No ads 👍.
  4. spellingbeeanswers.com Pretty formatting but not much else.
  5. nytspellinganswers.org Fancy narrative formatting.
  6. spellingbeetimes.com They have definitions – grabbed from somewhere – but not one for BIKING on 11/26/21? They even generate a silly youtube video!
  7. the-frenemy.com Other word puzzles as well.

Without a Subscription?

Is it illegal or unethical to play the NYT Spelling Bee puzzles without a subscription?

I do have a subscription to the Times. It is a reliable, trusted, and curated source of information. Subscriptions help the many journalists stay focused on their story.

The F command finds other dates that use the same 7 letters as in the current puzzle. These other puzzles usually (but not always) have a different center letter.

Archive

The archive was gathered mostly from nytbee.com and is added to daily at 3:00 a.m. EST by extracting the words from the New York Times site. There are daily puzzles going all the way back to May 29, 2018.

Which puzzles from the large archive to play? You can get one at random with the NR command or you could choose one with various attributes like ones with the most points or the fewest 4 letter words. Start the puzzle with the N command like so: 'N12-16-21'.
