Introduction

The New York Times has had the Spelling Bee puzzle in the print version of the weekly New York Times Magazine since 2014. The daily web version launched on May 9, 2018. Wikipedia has links to several stories about the game and the vibrant community of people who enjoy it. Many people play the game on their phone with the New York Times app.

Now there's another way to play - on the command line.

Here is a short film that shows how it works. There are several advantages to playing on the command line. Give it a try and let me know what you think.

How to Play

  • Create words using letters from the "hive".
  • Words must contain at least 4 letters.
  • Words must include the center letter.
  • Letters can be used more than once.

  • The list of allowed words does not include words that are obscure, hyphenated, or proper nouns.
  • No cussing either, sorry.

  • Score points to increase your rating.
  • 4-letter words are worth 1 point each.
  • Longer words earn 1 point per letter.
  • Each puzzle includes at least one "pangram" which uses every letter. These are worth 7 extra points!

If you haven't tried it before try it now on the web at nytimes.com.

Advantages of the Command Line

  • The table of hints is dynamic – it updates as you find words. It's also easier to access the table.
  • You can get dictionary definitions of puzzle words without revealing them. This is a fun way to challenge and enlarge your vocabulary.
  • Letters in the words can be slowly revealed giving another way to get hints.
  • You can play puzzles from the large NY Times archive. You can play forever (and do nothing else 😊).
  • You can play many puzzles and switch between them.
  • You are shown how many points you need to get to the next level.
  • You can clear a puzzle and play it again for practice.
  • You can play when offline - Airplane mode.
  • You can solve the full puzzle without a subscription to the Times.
  • There is a Pangram Mode where you only look for pangrams.
  • It is driven completely from the keyboard so is fast.

Commands

In addition to entering words that you have found there are also many single letter commands. Some commands have parameters. 

h  Show this Help
H  How to play
p  Permute the 6 non-center letters
w  Words found in alphabetical order
W  Words found in the order they were found
s  Score - including rank and number of assists
r  Rankings
a  Assist commands
o  Other commands
n  New puzzle from random date
l  List all current puzzles
n<num>  Switch to puzzle #num
P  Enter Pangram Mode
q  Quit - the words found will be remembered

Assist commands

t       Hints table and two letter hints
t1      Hints table
t2      Two letter hints
1       Random entry from the hints table
2       Random two letter hint
v 3 xy  Reveal 3 letters of words beginning with xy
v 4 x9  Reveal 4 letters of words of length 9
        beginning with x
v 1 p   Reveal 1 letter of the pangrams
d xy    Define words beginning with xy
d x6    Define words of length 6 beginning with x
d p     Define pangrams

There may not be definitions for every word.
Definitions are from Merriam-Webster, Collins or Oxford.
Definitions are not available when playing offline.

With d and v words already found are skipped.
Spaces in d and v commands are optional v4x9 = v 4 x9
The command e is like v but you'll see letters
at the beginning and End of the word.

Other commands

A     Download the Archive and pangrams for offline play.
n <date>   New puzzle from the given date - 10/20 or 2/5/19
lg    Show the timestamped log 25 lines at a time.
      Return goes to next page, q quits.
lga    Show the log with assists.
w <prefix> Show found words starting with a prefix.
k     Toggle clearing the screen or not.
      Useful to Keep text so you can scroll back.
d <word>   Define the word - no assist tallied.
D ... Like d but show all definitions instead of just 3.
      D also shows which dictionaries were used.
c     After confirmation Clear the puzzle and start afresh.
C     After confirmation Clear all saved puzzles.
g     After confirmation Give up and show all words.
x<num>  Remove (eXclude) puzzle #num.
R     Show Rankings with percentage of maximum.
S <word> Search for the word in the archive.
f     Find other puzzles with the same 7 letters.
L     List current puzzles with the 7 letters and center.
!...  Execute a system command - like 'date'.

Assist Strategy

Many people who solve the puzzle try to use as few hints (aka assists) as possible. The ultimate is to achieve QBABM = Queen Bee all by myself. This is possible but rather difficult. In this command line version there are many ways to get hints but with each hint your 'assist total' is increased. The number of assists are tallied like so: 
    Command   # of assists
t        15 but just once
t1       10 but just once
t2        5 but just once
d,D,v,e   1 for each word
1         1
2         1
g       100

Pangram Mode

The command P puts you into "pangram mode" where you search only for pangrams. There are different commands in this mode: 
    h    Show this help
p    Permute the 7 letters
d    Define the pangram(s)
v    Reveal another letter
e    Reveal another beginning and Ending letter
g    Give up and show the pangram(s)
q    Quit and return to Spelling Bee mode

The pangrams are chosen at random from a list of more than 1700 words that have exactly 7 unique letters in them. The list was derived from pangrams occuring in the NYT Bee puzzles plus a large lexicon of commonly used words in which the letter S is allowed.

Note: There may be multiple pangrams for a given set of 7 letters. However, not all valid pangrams are necessarily included. For example, ACELORT has electoral but not correllate or reallocate.
